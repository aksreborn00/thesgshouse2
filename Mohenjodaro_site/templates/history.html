History of Mohenjo-daro

Introduction

Mohenjo-daro, meaning "Mound of the Dead," is an archaeological site in the province of Sindh, Pakistan. Built around 2500 BCE, it was one of the largest settlements of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization and one of the world's earliest major cities.

Urban Planning and Architecture

The city was known for its advanced urban planning, including grid-like streets, elaborate drainage systems, and well-built houses. The Great Bath, a large public water tank, is one of its most remarkable structures.

Decline

Mohenjo-daro was abandoned around 1900 BCE for unknown reasons. Theories include climate change, tectonic activity, or invasion. The exact cause remains a subject of research and debate.
